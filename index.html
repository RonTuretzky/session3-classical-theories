Owen's model in detail

The recipe

  1. Reform the workplace first. 10.5h day instead of 14+. No child labor under 10. Education built into the workday for kids.
  2. Build the surrounding community. Cooperative store at near-cost. Infant school. Adult lectures. Music + dance + nature murals on classroom walls.
  3. Pay full wages during stoppages. 1806 cotton embargo: workers kept on payroll. Loyalty cemented.
  4. Scale to a "village of cooperation": 500–1,500 people on a parallelogram plan, agriculture + light industry combined, mostly self-sufficient.

Why it matters

Owen ran a profitable humane factory for 25 years. 20,000 visitors logged. Tsar Nicholas I considered taking 2 million Britons off Owen's hands.

The argument his model proves empirically: the brutality of industrial capitalism is not necessary for productivity. Every cooperative movement since (Rochdale Pioneers, Mondragón, kibbutz industries) rests on this single demonstration.

What New Harmony added (by failing): environmental design ≠ values vetting. Owen took anyone who showed up. In two years it collapsed. Every commune since has some absorption process as a result.

Ref: Owen, A New View of Society (1813)

The user asked for "a grand summary of what Owen's model was." This is it. Two-column: the recipe + why it matters. The Owen lesson is the most quoted in commune literature: design alone doesn't substitute for selection.

Place

North American Phalanx · Colts Neck, NJ · 1843–1856

The longest-lived Fourierist colony

Founded by Albert Brisbane (Fourier's chief American interpreter) with editorial backing from Horace Greeley, who put Fourierist columns on the front page of the New York Tribune from 1842 to 1844.

Faithful Series & Groups structure — task rotation through the day. Inverse-prestige pay: necessary > useful > agreeable work. Industrial diversification (grist mill, saw mill, the famous "Phalanx Tomato Catsup" sold in NYC).

Place · context first

Why the Icarian arc matters

Cabet's Voyage en Icarie (1840) was a runaway bestseller — by 1846 there were ~100,000 self-described Icarians in French cities. Cabet announced in 1847 he would actually build it. What followed is the most instructive long-arc lifecycle in 19th-century communalism.

Three structural lessons from 50 years of Icaria: (1) survive your founder (Cabet expelled 1856; the colonies that expelled him lasted 40 more years). (2) Geographic relocation is survivable — a commune is portable. (3) Print infrastructure (Le Populaire, 4,500 subscribers in 1846) was the proto-internet that built the movement.

Ref: Wikipedia · Icarians · Robert Sutton, Les Icariens (Illinois, 1994)

Anarcho-communism — the positive program

  • Property: abolished as private; means of production + consumption goods held in common.
  • Distribution: "from each according to ability, to each according to need" (past Bakunin's labor-token version).
  • Governance: direct democracy in the commune; federation across communes by recallable delegates.
  • Scale: communes the size of a quarter or village, federated regionally and globally.
  • Labor: 4–5 hours a day suffices once parasitism is abolished; rotation between manual and intellectual work.

In The Conquest of Bread, Kropotkin literally calculates how many work-hours Paris would need to feed and house itself. It is a startlingly concrete book.

The book that influenced the Spanish anarchists when they actually had to run things.

One example among many

The kibbutz: a 20th-century synthesis

One example of how Tolstoyan anarchism + Kropotkin's mutual aid + Landauer's communalism + labor Zionism converged into a working form.

Brief block. The kibbutz is interesting because it shows what happens when prior traditions fuse.

Where it came from — three threads converging

A.D. Gordon

Self-taught Russian estate manager. Arrived in Palestine at age 48 and joined orange-grove laborers. "Religion of labor."

Ber Borochov

Marxist labor Zionist. Theorized the inverted pyramid: a community severed from its productive base must rebuild it materially.

Martin Buber

Best friend of Landauer. Through Buber, European anarchism reached Palestine. Called the kibbutz "the experiment that did not fail."

Plus: Tolstoy and Kropotkin on every founder's reading list.

Refs: Gordon · Borochov · Buber, Paths in Utopia

Gordon = spiritual ground; Borochov = political-economic ground; Buber = philosophical ground. Plus heavy reading of Tolstoy and Kropotkin.

The arc, in one curve

~75 years of fully-collective form, then a long gradient toward differential salaries and renewed-kibbutz models. Both forms continue today.

An honest acknowledgment

The kibbutz movement was integral to the Zionist settlement project. Many kibbutzim were established on or near Palestinian villages whose inhabitants were displaced in 1948. A serious teaching has to hold both truths at once — the structural achievement, and the colonial context.

Refs: Gershon Shafir, Land, Labor and the Origins of the Israeli-Palestinian Conflict (Cambridge, 1989) · Zeev Sternhell, The Founding Myths of Israel (Princeton, 1998)
