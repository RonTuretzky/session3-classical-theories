Four "socialisms" we'll use precisely tonight

These words get conflated. Pin each one before we use it.

  • Utopian Socialism — pre-Marx designed colonies (Owen, Fourier, Cabet)
  • Mutualism — Proudhon's federations of cooperatives
  • Collectivist Anarchism — Bakunin: common production, distribution by labor
  • Anarchist Communism — Kropotkin: total common, distribution by need
Four terms, four following slides. Each has its own definition card.
Utopian Socialism
/juːˈtoʊpiən ˈsoʊʃəlɪzəm/ · noun · Owen / Fourier / Saint-Simon — 1810s–1830s
A designed alternative society described in concrete detail. Pre-Marx in subject — but the label was coined by Marx & Engels pejoratively.
Etymology. "Utopian socialism" is a Marxist coinage. Owen, Fourier, and Saint-Simon wrote in the 1810s–1830s — actively pre-Marx. Marx & Engels labeled them "utopian" pejoratively in the Communist Manifesto (1848) and Engels' Socialism: Utopian and Scientific (1880).
Engels, Manifesto §III.3 (1848): "They reject all political, and especially all revolutionary, action; they wish to attain their ends by peaceful means, necessarily doomed to failure, and by the force of example, to pave the way for the new social Gospel."
Method: build the model — the world will follow.
Examples: Owen (New Lanark, New Harmony) · Fourier (phalanstère) · Cabet (Icaria).

Note: "Pre-Marx" applies to the thinkers (chronologically); the label "utopian" is post-Marx (a polemic). The content predates the framing.

Refs: Wikipedia · Utopian socialism · Manifesto III.3 · Engels, Socialism: Utopian and Scientific

Mutualism
/ˈmjuːtʃuəlɪzəm/ · noun · Proudhon, 1840s
A society of independent producers — artisans, peasants, cooperatives — exchanging at cost via voluntary contracts, without bosses or interest. Federations of cooperatives replace the state.
Property: possession (use-rights) yes; property (rent extraction) no.
Financing: a People's Bank issuing interest-free credit to producers and cooperatives.
Famous formula: "Property is theft" — but "possession is liberty."
Real implementations: Paris Commune 1871 (early Proudhonist decrees: workers' management of abandoned workshops) · Jura Federation (Swiss watchmakers, 1872–1880s) · Mondragón Cooperative Corporation (Basque, 1956, ~70,000 worker-members) · Emilia-Romagna cooperative network (Italy, ~8,000 coops, ~30–40% of regional GDP) · Argentine empresas recuperadas (300+ worker-recovered factories, 2001–) · Community Land Trusts (1969–) · Sardex mutual credit (Sardinia, 2010–) · LETS (1983–).

Refs: Wikipedia · Mutualism · Proudhon, What is Property? · Mondragón · Sardex · Community Land Trusts

Real implementations enriched per user request: from Paris Commune to Mondragón to modern community land trusts.
Collectivist Anarchism
/kəˈlɛktɪvɪst ˈænərkɪzəm/ · noun · Bakunin, 1860s
Means of production held in common by workers' associations; distribution by labor contributed (not yet by need). Federation of communes via recallable delegates. No transitional state.

Spanish CNT — the largest real implementation

  • Confederación Nacional del Trabajo, founded November 1, 1910 in Barcelona at the Congreso de Solidaridad Obrera
  • Anarcho-syndicalist union — anarchism + syndicalism: general strike, industrial unions, no political parties, no paid officials
  • 1919: ~700,000 members; won the 8-hour day for all of Spain via the La Canadiense strike
  • 1934–36 peak: ~1.58 million members — briefly the largest union in Spain
  • July 1936: defeated Franco's coup in Barcelona; administered revolutionary Catalonia and Aragón
  • Nov 1936: Federica Montseny became Spain's first female cabinet minister
  • Affiliated body FAI (Federación Anarquista Ibérica, founded 1927 in Valencia)
  • Key figures: Buenaventura Durruti, Francisco Ascaso, Federica Montseny, Diego Abad de Santillán
  • Outlawed under Franco; refounded 1976. Today ~8,000 members in Spain, strongest in Catalonia

Refs: Wikipedia · CNT · FAI · Spanish Revolution 1936

Anarchist Communism
/ˈænərkɪst ˈkɒmjʊnɪzəm/ · noun · Kropotkin, 1880s
Total common ownership and distribution by need (not by labor). Federation of communes coordinates without commanding. 4–5h work/day suffices.

Anarchist Communism vs Collectivist Anarchism — why we cover both

Collectivism gives you a transition mechanism (pay people for what they do — solves the free-rider problem during scarcity); communism gives you the target (need-based in high-trust communities). Spain 1936 ran both simultaneously: communist within the village commune, collectivist across federations.

Two model-builders. Owen empirically demonstrated profitability of humane industry. Fourier designed the maximalist alternative on paper.

Owen's model — what they actually did

Industry: cotton-spinning. The River Clyde dropped fast enough at New Lanark to power four water-driven cotton mills. ~2,000 workers spun raw cotton into yarn, sold to weavers downstream.

The recipe

  1. Reform the workplace. 10.5h day instead of 14+. No child labor under 10. School built into the workday.
  2. Build the surrounding community. Co-op store at near-cost. Infant school (1816, first in Britain). Adult lectures. Music + dance + nature murals in classrooms.
  3. Pay full wages during stoppages. 1806 cotton embargo: workers kept on payroll. Loyalty cemented.
  4. Scale to a "village of cooperation": 500–1,500 people, parallelogram plan, agriculture + light industry combined.

Why it matters

Owen ran a profitable humane factory for 25 years. 20,000 visitors logged. Tsar Nicholas I considered taking 2 million Britons off his hands.

The argument: the brutality of industrial capitalism is not necessary for productivity. Every cooperative movement since (Rochdale Pioneers, Mondragón) rests on this single empirical demonstration.

The New Harmony lesson (1825 → 1827): environmental design ≠ values vetting. Owen took anyone. Collapsed in two years. Every commune since has some absorption process as a result.

Ref: Owen, A New View of Society (1813)

Owen's specifics: cotton spinning, water power, ~2,000 workers, 25 profitable years. The empirical demonstration is the foundational proof point of cooperative economics.

Owen's labor notes — the National Equitable Labour Exchange

London, September 1832 → 1834. Owen's most ambitious post-New-Lanark experiment — direct ancestor of every modern time-bank.

How it worked

  1. Craftsman brings finished good to the Exchange at Gray's Inn Road.
  2. Two valuators appraise it on (a) raw material cost + (b) labor-time required.
  3. Producer receives Labour Notes in denominations of 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 40, 80 hours — printed to look like banknotes.
  4. Producer walks the Exchange and buys other deposited goods, priced in hours.
  5. Branches: London + Birmingham + Liverpool + Glasgow.

What happened

  • Operated September 1832 → mid-1834 (about 18 months).
  • Adverse selection: shoemakers deposited hundreds of pairs of unsellable boots; producers walked off with notes, spent them on saleable goods.
  • No clearing mechanism between note-issuance and saleable inventory.
  • Owen's personal finances + rent dispute precipitated closure.
  • Antecedent: Josiah Warren's Cincinnati Time Store (May 1827 – 1830) ran the same idea five years earlier — Warren had been a New Harmony resident 1825–27.
Bequeaths: every modern time-bank. Edgar Cahn's Time Dollars (1980), TimeBanks USA (1995), Michael Linton's LETS (1983), Ithaca HOURS (1991) — direct lineage. The idea that the market can be replaced by exchange at labor-cost is the seed of modern alternative-currency design.

Fourier's phalanstère — model + scorecard

A single building for 1,620 souls (810 personality types × 2). The rue-galerie is the indoor "street" that connects everything — direct ancestor of the modern arcade and shopping mall.

Five-lens reading

How the building amplifies each lens

  • PROP (2): joint-stock association; members buy in. The building is jointly owned but private quarters preserved in the residence wing.
  • GOV (2): each Series elects its own foreman + delegates to a central council. The Court of the Phalanx is the assembly space.
  • LAB (4): the rue-galerie is the load-bearing structural innovation — it removes weather as friction so members can rotate through tasks every 2 hours without putting on a coat.
  • MEM (2): members are matched to Series by "passional type." Fourier calculated 810 personality types × 2 sexes = 1,620 souls.
  • OUT (2): the phalanstère is a demonstration — one of many that Fourier hoped would spread once anyone built the first.

Place — the one that got built

Familistère de Guise — Godin's 110-year phalanstère

The central pavilion of the Familistère de Guise — Godin's "Social Palace," the only Fourierist phalanstère actually built and the longest-lived (1859–1968). Wikimedia Commons

Jean-Baptiste André Godin (1817–1888), French industrialist who manufactured cast-iron stoves, was converted to Fourierism in his twenties. After Fourier's American disciples failed to build a real phalanstère, Godin used the profits of his stove factory in Guise (northern France) to do it himself. Construction began 1856; the first residential pavilion was completed 1859. By 1872 the Familistère housed ~900 workers and their families in three four-story residential blocks around glass-roofed interior courtyards — plus a nursery, infant school, primary school, theater, swimming pool, laundry, and cooperative shops. In 1880 Godin converted the entire enterprise — factory and Familistère together — to worker ownership through the Association coopérative du capital et du travail. The cooperative ran the foundry and the housing community until 1968 — 110 years.

The Familistère is the longest-lived classical communalist experiment of the 19th century and the only Fourier-derived community to make it past 1900.

Fourier's assumptions

  1. Passional productivity: properly channeled passion produces 3–4× wage labor
  2. Short rotations prevent burnout (the 2h cap)
  3. The Minimum eliminates poverty's drag on productivity
  4. Capped capital (4/12) aligns shareholders with workers
  5. 1,620 = 810 character types × 2 sexes — too small can't host all types

Linking to actual labor

  • Each member is simultaneously worker (5/12), shareholder (4/12), often specialist (3/12)
  • "Repugnance premium": hard work pays more per hour — voluntarily attracts members
  • Internal consumption isn't profit — it's the standard of living that justifies the Minimum
  • NAP (NJ, 1843–55) actually paid 4.4–5.6% dividends to capital under this scheme

Refs: Fourier, Le Nouveau monde industriel · Wikipedia · North American Phalanx

North American Phalanx · Colts Neck, NJ · 1843–1856

The Phalanstery building at Colts Neck, NJ — Historic American Buildings Survey photograph. Wikimedia Commons · PD-USGov

The longest-lived Fourierist colony

Founded by Albert Brisbane (Fourier's chief American interpreter) with backing from Horace Greeley, who put Fourierist columns on the front page of the New York Tribune 1842–1844.

Faithful Series & Groups structure. Task rotation through the day. Inverse-prestige pay: necessary > useful > agreeable. Industrial diversification — grist mill, saw mill, the famous "Phalanx Tomato Catsup" sold in NYC.

Lifecycle

1845 — Phalanstery built, peak ~125 members
1853 — religious/secular schism (Raritan Bay Union splits off)
1854 Nov — main mill burns
1856 — vote to dissolve, assets divided
Total: 12y 9m — longest secular Fourierist run.

Saint-Simon's ideas — the theory that built the modern world

He died in poverty in 1825 having built no commune. But his ideas became the headwaters of three traditions.

Core theory

  • Three-phases of history: theological → metaphysical → positive (scientific). Comte built positivism on this.
  • "Industriels" (productive workers, engineers, scientists, capitalists) vs "Oisifs" (idlers — clergy, aristocracy, rentiers).
  • Industrial Parliament: lower house of engineers, middle of scientists, upper of industrialists.
  • Famous formula: "From each according to capacity, to each capacity according to its works."

What disciples built

  • 1832 — Enfantin's Ménilmontant religious commune (blue uniforms, police raid)
  • 1833–1869 — Enfantin's Egyptian expedition + survey for the Suez Canal
  • 1852 — Pereire brothers found the Crédit Mobilier (first modern investment bank)
  • 1860 — Michel Chevalier negotiates the Cobden-Chevalier free trade treaty
  • 1869 — Ferdinand de Lesseps (disciple) opens the Suez Canal
  • Designed half the railways of the French Second Empire

Three living lineages

  • Technocracy — rule by technical experts (via Polytechnique → 20th-c managerialism)
  • Positivism + sociology — via Comte → Durkheim → social science as we know it
  • Finance-led development — Crédit Mobilier model → modern investment banking
  • Nouveau Christianisme (1825): secular religion, "society ought to organize itself to ameliorate the moral and physical existence of the poorest class."

      Anarcho-communism — the positive program

      Kropotkin's The Conquest of Bread (1892) is the operational manual. He literally calculates how many hours of work Paris would need to feed and house itself.

      Property

      Abolished as private. Means of production AND consumption goods held in common. The expropriation of bread is the first revolutionary act — guarantee that on day one, everyone eats.

      Labor

      4–5 hours/day, 20–25 years across a lifetime, suffices for everyone's needs (Kropotkin's actual calculation, Conquest of Bread Ch. 8). Rotation between manual + intellectual work.

      Federation

      Communes the size of a quarter or village, federated by recallable delegates. The delegate carries a bound mandate from her commune assembly; she cannot vote her conscience.

      Distribution by need — what that means precisely

      Not "everyone gets the same." It means:

      • The Minimum is universal. Bread, housing, clothing, heat — guaranteed to every member regardless of contribution. Same as Fourier's Minimum, but no member-share buy-in.
      • Use-rights, not ownership. You use what you need; you don't accumulate.
      • Voluntary contribution above the floor. Most adults contribute 4–5h/day because the alternative — refusing to work in a society of abundance — is socially rather than economically penalized.
      • Statistical, not bureaucratic. Kropotkin estimates aggregate needs (so many tons of bread for so many people) rather than rationing individuals.

      Recallable delegate — what makes it different

      A representative votes her conscience. A delegate votes her commune's instruction and can be replaced at any moment by the assembly that sent her. The commune is sovereign; the delegate is its messenger.

      Refs: Kropotkin, The Conquest of Bread (full text) · Kropotkin, Fields, Factories and Workshops
